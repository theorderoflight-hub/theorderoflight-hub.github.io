01

How This Document Works

Year Two does not replace Year One's brand system — it inherits it, and extends it. Read this document alongside the Year One Brand Bible, not instead of it.

Inheritance principle: every rule in the Year One Brand Bible remains in effect unless explicitly updated here. This document records what changes, what is added, and why. Anything not mentioned is unchanged.

The Year Two identity is the same identity under pressure. The mark is unchanged. The typefaces are unchanged. The core palette is unchanged. What Year Two adds is a new frequency — deep blue, the colour of depth and signal travelling through water — and a heavier treatment of degradation across all visual assets.

The guiding principle: same world, more worn. The grain sits heavier. The damage is more present. The signal still arrives — it is simply more visibly worn by the distance it has traveled. Nothing is broken. Everything has been in use.

The band name The Order of Light is set in DM Sans throughout — on covers, in typography specimens, in the wordmark, in all public-facing materials. Cormorant Garamond remains the serif layer for titles, taglines, pull quotes, and display — but never for the band name itself.

Year One — What Was
Grain: subtle, controlled — 0.04 opacity
Scan lines: absent
Palette: void, crimson, gold
Glitch: punctuation — applied sparingly
Broadcasts: open with static, structured sections
Radial glow: warm, gold-tinted
Conduit: certain, declarative
Year Two — What Changes
Grain: heavier, more persistent — 0.065 opacity
Scan lines: present across all assets
Palette: void, crimson, gold + deep blue + ice
Glitch: background condition — more persistent
Broadcasts: open mid-transmission, sections bleed
Radial glow: cooler, blue-shifted
Conduit: examines, uncertain in earned ways

Colour Palette — Year Two

The Year One palette is inherited unchanged. Year Two adds deep blue and ice as the new frequency — the colour of depth, pressure, and signal traveling through water. These new colours do not replace the existing palette. They enter it as an undertone.

The three-pole palette: gold (the warmth of the original signal), crimson (the urgency of transmission), deep blue (the cold depth of where the signal has gone). The interplay between all three across a year of content is the visual story of Year Two.

Do
Use deep blue as the leading colour for Year Two section headers, key typography, and the accent line on Broadcasts. It signals to the audience that something has changed without explaining it.
Do
Reserve gold for warmth and memory — moments that reference the past, The Frequency materials, anything that predates the deepening. Gold is now the colour of what was.
Don't
Do not use deep blue and gold together prominently in the same composition. They represent opposite poles — past warmth and present depth. When they appear together the tension should be intentional and controlled.
Don't
Do not introduce warm backgrounds — no navy gradients, no teal, no purple. The depth implied by deep blue is cold. Warmth undermines it.
Note
The mark remains unchanged in colour — it can be rendered in any of the palette's colours as it was in Year One, with the addition of deep blue as a new valid mark colour for Year Two-specific contexts.

Typography

The typeface system is unchanged — DM Sans and Cormorant Garamond. The application shifts in Year Two: ice replaces gold as the primary label and navigation colour, gold is reserved for warmth and memory contexts. The band name is DM Sans throughout without exception.

The Order of Light is always set in DM Sans, weight 200, tracked wide. This applies to the cover, to typography specimens, to all public-facing materials, to all internal documents. Cormorant Garamond is never used for the band name. Cormorant remains for display titles, taglines, pull quotes, and Broadcast script styling only.

Note
The two-typeface rule remains absolute. No third typeface enters the system in Year Two. The added depth comes from colour and degradation, not from typographic variety.

The Mark — Year Two

The mark geometry is unchanged — the asymmetric eight-pointed form, 19-unit gaps between cardinal and diagonal rays, thicker X diagonals. What changes is the treatment context and the addition of deep blue as a valid mark colour.

Geometry is locked. Cardinal rays longer. Diagonal rays slightly shorter. X diagonals at stroke-width 8.5. Cardinal rays at stroke-width 7. 19-unit gaps. These proportions do not change in Year Two. The mark is not redesigned — it is re-contextualised.

Glitch Grammar — Year Two

Year One's Glitch Grammar is inherited and extended. The techniques are the same. The application is heavier — glitch moves from punctuation to background condition. It is no longer occasional. It is the persistent evidence of wear.

Year One grammar inherited: VHS tracking errors, chromatic aberration, pitch drop, static hold, and tape splice all remain in use. Frequency of application increases across all. The signal still arrives — it is simply more visibly worn.
01
Persistent Chromatic Aberration
Year One: chromatic aberration increased briefly, then settled. Year Two: it is present at a low level throughout — not dramatic, subliminal. The RGB channels are very slightly misaligned at all times. Punctuation moments push it further.
Always present · Punctuation: 3× per Broadcast
02
Heavier Grain
Grain opacity increased from 0.04 to 0.065 across all static assets. In motion content, grain is more visible during moments of thematic weight — not applied randomly, but with the same intentionality as Year One's tracking errors.
Always present · All assets
03
VHS Tracking — Extended Duration
Year One: tracking errors lasted 2–3 frames. Year Two: up to 8–10 frames before recovery. The signal takes longer to stabilise. Apply once in opening section, once in the Message, once in the Close — never more than three times per Broadcast.
3× per Broadcast · max 10 frames each
04
Pitch Drop — Retained
Inherited unchanged from Year One. Audio drops approximately a semitone over 2–3 seconds during the Message section. Applied once per Broadcast. Most viewers will feel it rather than hear it. The audio cue that something is traveling from further away.
Once per Broadcast · Message section
05
Scan Lines
New in Year Two. A persistent horizontal scan line pattern across all video assets — barely visible at normal viewing size, present on close inspection. Applied to static images as well. Reinforces the sense that everything is being received through an imperfect medium.
Always present · All assets
06
The Second Figure
New in Year Two. From Broadcast 002: a second figure, partial, at the edge of frame. Never addressed. Never explained. Appears three times across the five Broadcasts. Not a glitch technique — a narrative element introduced through visual grammar. The audience will find it. Let them.
Broadcasts 002, 004, 005 · Edge of frame only
06

Year Two Rules — Complete

All Year One rules remain in effect. The following are Year Two-specific rules and updates. Where a rule conflicts with Year One, Year Two takes precedence for Year Two content.

Never
Never set the band name in Cormorant Garamond. DM Sans weight 200, tracked wide, is the only acceptable wordmark treatment. This overrides any conflicting usage from Year One materials.
Do
Use deep blue as the leading accent colour for Year Two. Section headers, navigation labels, Broadcast kickers, the accent line on covers — wherever crimson led in Year One, deep blue leads in Year Two.
Do
Use gold only for warmth and memory. The Frequency materials, moments referencing Year One, anything that carries the past forward. Gold is no longer the primary accent — it is the accent of before.
Never
Never introduce warm backgrounds. The void remains the primary background. Deep blue enters as a surface tint (rgba, low opacity) — never as a solid field. The world is still dark.
Note
The record title Below the Line of Sight is a working title and may change before release. All brand materials referencing the record title should treat it as provisional until confirmed.
Do
Open Broadcasts mid-transmission. No static opening. The Conduit is already speaking when you arrive. This is the single most important structural change from Year One to Year Two — it reframes the nature of the broadcast from event to fragment of something continuous.
Never
Never explain the second figure. It appears three times. It is never addressed in any Broadcast, in any social copy, in any press material. If asked directly, the answer is silence or deflection. The audience who finds it should feel they found something real.
Never
Never use three typefaces. DM Sans and Cormorant Garamond remain the complete type system. No additions in Year Two. The depth comes from colour and degradation — not typographic variety.
Do
Allow the brand to feel worn. Year Two assets should not look freshly made. The grain is heavier, the scan lines are present, the chromatic aberration is persistent. This is not careless production — it is deliberate. The signal has been traveling for a year.
Note
