Campaign Thesis

Year One asked the audience to receive the signal. Year Two asks what receiving it has done to them.

Year One built the world. Year Two deepens it. The campaign does not reintroduce the project — it continues it. The audience who followed Year One arrives at Year Two having been changed by it. The campaign treats them as already inside the mythology, not as new arrivals being welcomed in.
The signal has been traveling. Visually, tonally, and narratively, Year Two carries evidence of distance. The grain is heavier. The Conduit has changed. The palette has deepened. None of this is announced. The audience who notices is the audience this project is for.
Do
Return without announcement. Year Two does not begin with a launch post. The signal returns — a single image, a changed aesthetic — and the audience finds it. This mirrors Year One's mythology-first strategy.
Do
Let the record title remain provisional. Below the Line of Sight is the working title. Campaign materials can reference the record without naming it until the official announcement window.
Don't
Don't explain the gap between years. No "we've been busy" posts. No behind-the-scenes content during the silence period. The mythology does not acknowledge real-world logistics.

05

The Frequency — Year Two Execution

Same structure. Different song. The series proves itself through repetition. The second year, the audience already knows what it is — which changes everything.

The song: Written from the record's thematic world — Memory, Depth, Transformation. The collaborators receive it without knowing the record's full context. What they do with it comes entirely from what the song gives them.

Collaborator selection: Year Two expands the pool. Consider reaching outside alternative rock — folk, post-rock, ambient, or art-pop. The comparison between versions is more revealing when the interpretive distance between collaborators is greater.

The Source: Distributed in Months 5–6. Same format as Year One — chart, lyric sheet, The Rules, the embargo clause. The collaborators are given the same constraints. The outcome differs because the people differ.

Release sequence: Transmission I in Month 7 (established collaborator, same week as the record). Transmission II in Month 8–9. Transmission III in Month 9–10. Minimum two-week gap between transmissions. The audience has time to sit with each before the next arrives.

The Illuminated: Founding members receive the original version before any Transmission releases. This is the one point where the audience hears the song before it's been reinterpreted. That listening is private and unrecorded.

Do
Release Frequency Transmission I in the same week as the record. The collision of the two release events amplifies both.
Do
Let the three versions exist publicly at the same time by Month 10. The comparison is the content. Don't rush the spacing just to get them all out.
Don't
Don't reveal collaborators in advance. The announcement is the release. Each Transmission drops with the collaborator's name on the day it drops — not before.

Campaign Rules — Year Two

Year One rules are inherited. Year Two adds and updates. Where a rule conflicts, Year Two takes precedence for Year Two content.

Never
Never explain the second figure. It appears three times across five Broadcasts. It is never captioned, hashtagged, or addressed in any post. If the audience asks directly, silence or deflection. What they found is theirs.
Do
Treat the record release and Frequency Transmission I as a single event. The same week. The audience is given two things to hold at once — the complete work and the first interpretation of the work's seed.
Never
Never post "Year Two is here" copy. The campaign is already underway when the audience returns. The signal doesn't announce itself.
Do
Use deep blue as the leading visual signal of Year Two. The palette shift is the campaign's visual thesis. It should be consistent across all assets from Month 1 onward.
Note
The record title is provisional. Below the Line of Sight is the working title. Do not lock campaign materials to this title until the official announcement. Refer to "the record" or "the full-length" in internal documents until the name is confirmed.
Do
Honour the Illuminated first, always. Every major announcement and release event should reach Illuminated members before the general public — even by a few hours. The community's trust is the campaign's most valuable asset.
Never
Never break the mythology frame on social media. No casual "thanks for the support" posts. No real-name mentions. No behind-the-scenes content that reads as a normal band. The Order of Light does not exist outside its world.
