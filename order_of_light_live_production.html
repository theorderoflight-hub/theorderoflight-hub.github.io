01

Show Philosophy

A live show is not a performance of the recorded material — it's a transmission of it. The two things are different. A performance reproduces. A transmission delivers something to the room that wasn't there before. That distinction should inform every decision about how the show is built and run.

The Conduit doesn't appear on stage. The band is the physical signal the Conduit has been broadcasting — five people locked together, playing songs that mean something. That's it. No mythology theatre, no complicated narrative staging. The world is built before the audience arrives. The job of the show is to make the music as good as it can possibly be.

Between songs: say less. A ten-second pause is always better than ninety seconds of stage talk. If something needs to be said, say it plainly and move on. The music is the message. Anything said from stage should serve the music, not explain it.

No encore. The show ends when the last song ends. The blackout is the ending. Playing a false exit and coming back out is a performance convention — skip it.

05

Lighting Brief

Send to the LD before load-in. This is a brief, not a rigid spec — the LD has creative input within these parameters.

Colour palette
Deep red/crimson and warm amber. No blues, greens, or whites except as rare punctuation. The stage should feel like the inside of the visual world — dark, warm at the edges.
Base wash
Very low-level amber wash across the whole stage. Musicians visible, not illuminated. Darkness is the default state.
Throughout — all songs
Vocal spot
Tight spot on the vocalist — warm amber, soft edge. Not a harsh theatrical spot. Lit from within the darkness rather than spotlit from above.
Vocal moments, choruses
Crimson pulse
On heavy rhythmic accents — a brief red flash across the full stage. Not a strobe. Used sparingly, maximum 3–4 times per song on the biggest moments only.
Song climaxes — cued to BPM
Pre-show
Stage fully dark. The eight-pointed mark on the rear wall if screen/gobo is available. Changeover tone through PA. The audience arrives to an already-present world.
Stage clear through first note
Post-show
Last note — immediate full blackout. No walk-off lighting, no bow. 5–10 seconds of complete darkness before house lights. The transmission ends without ceremony.
Final note → instant black
06

Set List Philosophy

A set list is a transmission arc — it has an opening, a middle, a climax, and an ending. Every song has a position. The audience should feel the set going somewhere even if they can't name where.

Start hard. Don't dip too early — earn the quieter moments. Build. The last song should be the biggest moment in the room. Then: immediate blackout.
