The Brief

This band is a brand. The merch is the physical proof of that. Every object needs to feel like it came from the world of The Order of Light — not like it was made to sell at a merch table. The standard is elevated basics: quality materials, precise printing, restrained design. The kind of thing you wear because it's genuinely good, not because it has a band name on it.

Small runs. High quality. Limited availability. The scarcity isn't artificial — it reflects the reality of a band that values craft over volume. If something sells out and people ask for it again, that's a success, not a problem.

The test: Every piece should pass the "would I wear this if I didn't know the band?" test. If the answer is no, the design or the quality isn't there yet.
02

Garment Tag — Inside Label

Following the alltheprettythings model — the mark on the hem, a tagline on the inside label. The tagline should feel like it belongs to the world, not to marketing. Options to react to:

Preferred direction
From within the signal.
Minimal. Mythological without being precious. Sounds like something you'd find on a document from inside the world — not a marketing line.
Direction B
Carry the frequency.
Direct. Ties to The Frequency framework. Action-oriented — you're doing something by wearing it. Slightly more declarative.
Direction C
The transmission continues.
References the ongoing nature of the project. Good for a label because it's present-tense — the work isn't finished. Could date oddly.
Direction D — open
Write your own here.
All four directions are starting points. The right one will feel inevitable when it lands. It should work on a 1cm hem tag in 6pt type and still mean something.
Tag structure: Outside hem — the mark (eight-pointed, 10mm, printed or woven). Inside neck label — band name in DM Sans weight 200 + tagline in DM Mono 8pt + care/composition info. Same quality as the garment. Not an afterthought.

01

Philosophy

The Illuminated is the inner community of The Order of Light. It is designed as a genuine creative community — not a revenue stream disguised as one. The distinction matters: members pay to be closer to the work, not to be marketed at from a shorter distance.

Everything the Illuminated receives — early access, exclusive content, physical objects — should feel like it was made for them specifically. The mythology of the project makes this natural: they are not subscribers, they are members of an order. That framing should be consistent across every touchpoint.

The founding cap of 108 is not arbitrary — it is the mythology's central number, and it makes the founding cohort genuinely finite. Once 108 founding memberships are issued, they cannot be reissued. This is not a marketing trick. It is a commitment to the people who arrived first.

06

Black rigid mailer or folding box. The mark and member number printed on the exterior. Inside: black tissue, sealed with the wax-impression sticker. The unboxing is part of the mythology.
Production note: source quotes for print and enamel manufacturing before confirming the $108 price point. Recommend using a print-on-demand certificate printer for the numbered certificates to avoid holding stock. Budget approximately $30–45 per kit all-in at 108 units.

Platform Recommendation

Recommended
Memberful
Integrates directly with Stripe (lowest fees), allows custom branding, supports Discord role integration natively, and lets you host content directly. No marketplace — members join through your own site. 4.9% + $0.30 per transaction. Most professional option for a project that wants to control its own presentation.
Alternative
Patreon
Higher discoverability, established audience familiarity with the format. 8% platform fee on the Pro plan. Built-in Discord integration. Lower control over branding and presentation — members see the Patreon interface, not yours. Acceptable if launching quickly is the priority.
Not recommended
Bandcamp subscriptions
Limited to music and downloads. No Discord integration, no physical tier support, no community features. Fine as an additional channel but not a primary membership platform.

Launch Sequence

Merch doesn't launch all at once. It arrives in phases tied to the band's public existence.

Pre-launch
Ordination Kit only
The ordination kit is the only physical object available. Includes enamel pin and numbered print. Founding membership begins here. No general merch store yet — this keeps it exclusive.
EP launch
Store opens — core garments + mints
Tee, long sleeve, tote, mints tin go live simultaneously with the EP release. Illuminated members get 48hr early access. The mints are the launch object — handed out at the first show, circulated as the moment piece.
First show
Merch table: curated, not exhaustive
3–4 items max at the merch table. Tee, crewneck or long sleeve, pin, mints. The table should look considered, not like a clearance rack. Quality over quantity at every visible touchpoint.
End of Year One
Coach jacket — limited cycle drop
The outerwear piece drops as a year-end / holiday exclusive. Illuminated members get first access. 50–75 units. Does not restock. This becomes the collector's piece for the first era.
