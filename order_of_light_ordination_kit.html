01

Philosophy

The Ordination Kit is not merchandise. It is an artefact — a physical object that makes the mythology real in someone's hands.

The difference between merch and an artefact: merch announces. An artefact initiates. A person who opens the Ordination Kit should feel they have been given something — admitted to something — not that they have bought something. Every design and production decision flows from this distinction.
The kit must look like it belongs to the world. The brand system is the design brief. Void black, crimson, gold. The mark. DM Sans and Cormorant Garamond only. Grain texture where appropriate. The object should feel discovered, not manufactured.

The kit ships to Illuminated members who join at the Founding Tier level. Standard Tier members can add it to their membership at cost. Year Two pre-orders include a Year Two variant as a bundle option.

The kit is not a box of tchotchkes. It is a small number of objects, each meaningful, each made well. Better to ship three things that feel special than eight things that feel random. Quality over quantity without exception.

02

Kit Contents — Standard

Six objects. Each serves the mythology. None is purely decorative.

Core
01
The Ordination Card
Heavy card stock — 600gsm minimum. Void black. The mark foil-stamped in crimson or gold. The recipient's ordination number letterpress-printed. A single line of copy: "You are now inside the signal." Packaged separately within the kit — the first object encountered on opening.
Size: 90×55mm (standard card). Unique numbered per recipient from 001 upward. Founding members receive the lowest numbers.
Core
02
The Mark Pin
Hard enamel pin. The eight-pointed mark. 30mm width. Black fill, crimson enamel on X diagonals, gold enamel on cardinal rays. Black rubber clutch backs. The mark as a wearable object — subtle enough that only those who know will recognise it.
Supplier: Vograce, Pintrill, or equivalent. Min. order: 100 units. Lead time: 3–4 weeks. Estimate: $4–6 per unit at 100.
Core
03
The Transmission Print
A5 giclée art print. The mark large, centered on void black. A fragment of Broadcast copy beneath it in DM Sans — a line that does not appear in the public-facing Broadcasts. Signed in the mythology — not by a band member name, but as "Transmitted." Matte finish. Sealed in a glassine envelope inside the kit.
Print-on-demand via Printful or local print house. A5 (148×210mm). Min. 180gsm. Run to order — no excess stock.
Optional
04
The 108 Wax Seal Sheet
A sheet of 6 custom wax seal stickers — the mark rendered as a wax seal impression in deep red on ivory paper. Sticker-backed for use on envelopes, notebooks, instruments. A reference to the mythology's numerological foundation. The stickers are the most giftable item in the kit — useful, visual, and branded without being loud.
Custom sticker sheets via StickerMule or similar. Sheet size: 100×150mm. Cost: ~$2–3/sheet at 100+.
Core
05
The Welcome Scroll
A folded A4 document — the mythology's admission document. Not a thank-you note. A transmission. Printed on aged-looking cream stock (not white). Cormorant Garamond body, DM Sans labels. Copy describes what the recipient is now part of, what The Illuminated is, and what to expect. Contains the Discord/community access code. Rolled and tied, or folded and sealed with the wax seal sticker.
In-house print or short-run letterpress. Sealed with kit's wax seal sticker — the seal is broken when opened. One per kit.
Year Two
06
Record Insert (Year Two Bundle)
Added to Year Two pre-order bundle kits only. A folded insert card specific to the full-length record — the record artwork on one side, the tracklist in Cormorant Garamond on the reverse. Printed on the same stock as the Ordination Card. A Year Two-specific artefact that marks the transition from EP arc to full-length.
Year Two variant only. Not included in Year One Ordination Kit. Add to Year Two pre-order bundle at same point as kit.
03

Outer Packaging

The box is part of the artefact. The experience begins the moment the kit is held.

Box Type
Rigid two-piece lift-off lid box. Black board — not printed on the outside. The mark hot-foil stamped on the lid in gold, centered. Nothing else on the exterior. The restraint is the point.
Size
Approximately 160×120×40mm. Large enough to hold all contents with dignity — not crammed in. Contents should sit, not be stuffed.
Interior
Black foam insert or black tissue paper. The Ordination Card placed on top, face up — the first thing seen when the lid is removed. Other items beneath or alongside, the print sealed in its glassine envelope.
Exterior seal
The box is sealed with a single wax seal sticker — the same one used on the Welcome Scroll. Breaking the seal is the act of entry. The mythology begins before the box is open.
Supplier
PakFactory, BoxUp, Packlane, or local rigid box supplier. Request samples before committing to print run. Confirm foil stamp capability. Min. order: typically 50–100 units.

Fulfillment

The kit ships from home or a local fulfillment point in Year One. Year Two bundle fulfillment may require third-party logistics if order volume warrants it.

Sales platform
Bandcamp (preferred for physical + digital bundles and low fees) or Shopify. Both allow custom order confirmation emails — use these to continue the mythology. The order confirmation is a transmission, not a receipt.
Shipping
USPS Priority Mail for domestic. Flat-rate boxes where possible. Do not use generic shipping boxes — the outer mailer should also be branded. A plain black kraft mailer with the mark rubber-stamped is sufficient and low cost.
Order confirmation
The confirmation email continues the mythology. Not "Thanks for your order" — something like "Your ordination is in transmission." Subject line: "Your number has been assigned." The email experience is part of the product.
Year Two scale
If Year Two pre-order volume exceeds 100 units for the bundle, consider Printful or ShipBob for fulfillment. At this scale the print and pin still require pre-production; only the fulfillment is outsourced.
07

Rules

Do
Get samples before full production runs. Every item. No exceptions. A pin that looks slightly off at scale is an artefact that undermines the mythology every time someone looks at it.
Do
Number the Ordination Cards sequentially from 001. The founding members with the lowest numbers are the mythology's earliest witnesses. That fact carries weight and should be named explicitly in the Welcome Scroll.
Never
Never use white or light packaging stock. The kit exists in the void-and-crimson world of the brand. Every piece of paper, every envelope, every box is dark, muted, or black. A white packing slip breaks the artefact experience immediately.
Never
Never include anything that reads as generic merchandise. No band tee in the kit. No CD tucked in as an afterthought. The kit is curated — every object earns its place or it is cut.
Note
The Welcome Scroll is a living document. Update it between Year One and Year Two to reflect the mythology's progression. Founding members get the Year One version; new Year Two members get the updated version. Keep archived copies of each.
Do
Treat the confirmation email as content. The order experience is part of the membership. Write the confirmation email in the mythology's voice before the first kit ships. Test it against a non-fan — if they can tell it came from a band, rewrite it.
