Overview

A Broadcast is a short video — typically 90 seconds to 3 minutes — in which The Conduit delivers a transmission. It is the mythology's public face and the project's primary content format. The visual treatment must be consistent across all five Year One Broadcasts. This guide covers the complete production workflow from finished script to published video.

The Conduit is generated via HeyGen using a custom AI avatar. Post-production happens in DaVinci Resolve. The Glitch Grammar documented in the Brand Bible is applied at the post stage. Each Broadcast takes approximately 3–5 hours of production time once the workflow is established.

Year One vs Year Two: Year One Broadcasts open with static, then the Conduit appears. Year Two Broadcasts open mid-transmission — no static opening. This guide covers Year One production. The Year Two Brand Bible documents the changes needed for Year Two.

Production Workflow

Eight stages from script to published. Follow in sequence — skipping stages causes rework.

Stage 1 — Prep
Script review and lock

Confirm the script is final — not a draft. HeyGen generation is not cheap to redo. Read the script aloud at the intended delivery pace to confirm timing (target: 90s–3min). Check all section markers are correct: Opening / Message / The Call / End Card.

Year One note: the script opens with a static hold cue. This is not spoken — it is a production instruction for post. Mark it clearly in the script document as [STATIC — 4 seconds] before line one.

Stage 2 — HeyGen generation
Generate base video from script

In HeyGen: select The Conduit avatar. Paste the script text (excluding the static cue). Set background to solid void black (#0A0A0A). Resolution: 1080×1920 (portrait/story) as the master — this crops to 1080×1080 and 1920×1080 without reshot.

Voice: use the custom Conduit voice model. Delivery pace: slow and deliberate — if the platform generates it too fast, use the speed control (0.85–0.9× is usually correct). Preview before committing credits.

Export as MP4, highest available quality. This is the raw base file — do not publish this directly.

Stage 3 — Audio post
Process voice audio

Extract audio from the HeyGen export. In Audacity or Audition:

Pitch drop: in the Message section — locate the target moment, apply a gradual pitch shift of approximately −1 semitone over 2–3 seconds, recovering back to normal pitch. Subtle — if it's immediately noticeable, it's too much.

Ambient layer: add a low-level sine tone at 432Hz or 528Hz beneath the voice — nearly inaudible (−30dB), present under silence. This is the frequency hum. Fade in at the opening, fade out at the end card.

Export processed audio as WAV, 48kHz/24bit. Replace audio in DaVinci Resolve in stage 5.

Stage 4 — DaVinci Resolve import
Set up project and import assets

New project in DaVinci Resolve. Timeline settings: 1080×1920, 24fps (or 30fps — keep consistent across all Broadcasts). Import the HeyGen MP4, the processed WAV, and the static asset (a generated static/noise clip — create once, reuse).

Colour space: DaVinci Wide Gamut, Intermediate. Master the final grade in Rec.709 for export.

Replace the HeyGen audio track with the processed WAV from Stage 3.

Stage 5 — Colour grade
Apply the Year One visual treatment

Lift/Gamma/Gain: crush blacks hard (lift −0.05), reduce saturation to approximately 70%, add a very slight warm tint in the shadows (orange/red, subtle). The image should feel dark, intimate, and slightly overexposed in the highlights.

Grain: add Resolve's built-in Film Grain at strength 0.3–0.4. Size: Fine. This is the base grain layer — the Brand Bible's 0.04 opacity level.

Vignette: moderate circular vignette, centred on The Conduit's face. Not heavy — the edges should darken, not go black.

Stage 6 — Glitch Grammar
Apply distortion effects per Brand Bible spec

Apply each glitch element in sequence. See the checklist in Section 04 of this document for the complete spec with timings.

Stage 7 — Titles and end card
Add text overlays and end card

Opening title: after the static hold, before The Conduit speaks — "BROADCAST 00[X]" in DM Sans ExtraLight, tracked at 0.4em, white, centre frame, 1.5 seconds, fade out. Small — not dominant.

End card: The Conduit finishes speaking. Cut to void black. The eight-pointed mark appears at centre, fades in over 1 second. Holds 2 seconds. Fades out. "THE ORDER OF LIGHT" in DM Sans ExtraLight below the mark, tracked wide, appears with the mark. That is the end. No call to action. No link. No follow prompt.

Stage 8 — Export and publish
Export per platform and schedule

Export master file, then platform-specific versions. See Section 05 for platform export specs. Review the final export on a phone before publishing — the primary viewing context is mobile.

04

Glitch Grammar — Production Checklist

Apply all of these. In this order. Every Broadcast — no exceptions.

01
Static hold opening — 4 seconds of VHS static before The Conduit appears. Use the static asset. Cut hard from static to Conduit — no cross-fade. The Conduit begins speaking immediately.
Seconds 0–4
02
Chromatic aberration — first hit — RGB channel split, 3–4 frames. In Resolve: use the Chromatic Aberration effect or offset R/B channels manually on a Fusion node. Offset: 4–6px. Apply once in the first 20 seconds.
First 20s
03
VHS tracking error — first — horizontal band distortion, 2–3 frames. Use a Resolve Transform node with a quick horizontal position offset (+40px for 1 frame, back) plus a brief desaturation. Feels like tape slipping.
Opening section
04
Pitch drop — audio only, applied in Stage 3. Confirm it is audible (but subtle) in the mix before finalising. Occurs once, in the Message section.
Message section
05
VHS tracking error — second — same technique as above. Apply at a moment of thematic weight in the Message. Slightly longer than the first — 3 frames. The signal straining under the content.
Message section
06
Chromatic aberration — second hit — apply once more, at or near The Call section opening. Same technique as the first. This is the final glitch before the end card.
The Call / late
07
Brief static flash — close — 1–2 frames of static, then back to normal, approximately 5 seconds before The Conduit finishes speaking. Not a full static hold — just a flash. The signal flickering before transmission ends.
Near end
08
Review total glitch count — the above gives 2 chromatic aberration hits, 2 VHS tracking errors, 1 static flash, 1 pitch drop. That is the maximum. If any of these feel heavy or noticeable on first viewing, pull back — subtlety is the standard.
Review
The cardinal rule: every glitch should be felt before it is noticed. If a viewer's first reaction is "that's a glitch effect," it's too much. Their first reaction should be a vague unease they cannot immediately name.

Pre-Publication Checklist

Review every item before scheduling. On a phone, not a monitor.

☐Static hold is exactly 4 seconds at the opening
☐Opening title reads correctly — BROADCAST 00[X], correct number
☐All 8 glitch grammar elements applied and confirmed subtle
☐Pitch drop audible but not jarring — felt, not noticed
☐End card: mark appears cleanly, no artefacts, correct fade
☐Reviewed on mobile — The Conduit is visible, titles are in safe zone
☐Audio levels consistent — no clipping, voice clear, ambient layer inaudible at normal volume
☐Caption written and reviewed — Broadcast number, tagline, no hashtag spam
☐YouTube thumbnail: the mark on void black. No other thumbnail.
☐Scheduled — not posted immediately. Even if ready now, schedule 24 hours out. You will notice something you want to change.
