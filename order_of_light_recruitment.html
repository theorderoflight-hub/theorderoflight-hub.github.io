Vocalist · Charlotte NC
The signal needs
a voice.
Lead vocalist & co-writer sought for serious original project

We are building something deliberate and unhurried. A rock band with real melodic ambition — the kind of music that rewards close listening and doesn't apologise for wanting to move people. We write songs that breathe, groove, and stay with you. Atmospherically dark but emotionally warm. The signal is in place. We need the voice that carries it.

If your reference points include artists like Thrice, Death Cab for Cutie, Copeland, Nothing But Thieves, Counting Crows, Young the Giant, and Mew — and if you hear those influences not as a genre checklist but as a shared sensibility — we may be looking for each other.

What this requires
  • Strong lead vocals with genuine range — tone, dynamics, and emotional control
  • Harmony ability is non-negotiable. Stacked vocal arrangements are core to our sound
  • Previous studio recording experience — you know how you sound on tape
  • Live touring history — you know what it means to perform consistently night after night
  • Co-writing capability — lyrics, melody, or both. We have extensive material but this is collaborative
  • The confidence to submit original material and write to existing tracks as part of the audition
  • Based in Charlotte, NC or genuinely willing to be here for rehearsals. This is in-person
This is not the project for you if —

You're looking to join something casual. If you treat music as an occasional hobby, have no recording or touring experience to show, or if you're not comfortable being part of a project with a strong defined aesthetic and artistic vision, we'll both know quickly that it isn't right. We'd rather be honest about that upfront.

Age range: 30s–40s preferred. This band holds one non-negotiable: every human being deserves equal dignity regardless of who they are, who they love, or where they come from. If that's a political statement to you, we're genuinely not the right project.

Contact: tristanmatthew@gmail.com · Include links to recordings and a brief introduction

Drummer · Charlotte NC
The signal needs
a pulse.
Drummer sought for serious original project with forward momentum

We are not looking for someone who plays loud. We are looking for someone who plays right. The drumming this project needs is rooted in feel, pocket, and an understanding that restraint is a musical decision — and that when it's time to open up, that means something. Atmospheric and dynamic. Groove-forward. Unwilling to settle for adequate.

If you find yourself reaching for records by MuteMath, Minus The Bear, Sunny Day Real Estate, Thrice, Hell Is For Heroes or Mew when you want to hear drumming that serves the song rather than competing with it — you already understand what we're after.

What this requires
  • Technical ability matched by taste — you know when to fill and when to lock in and breathe
  • Proven studio experience — you've tracked to a click, worked with a producer, heard yourself back
  • Touring history — road experience, not just weekend shows
  • The ability to sing harmony. This band layers vocals. Willingness to contribute is essential
  • Comfortable working with electronic elements and loops as part of the rhythm foundation
  • Ability to learn existing material and demonstrate chemistry in person as part of the process
  • Charlotte, NC based or genuinely available for regular in-person rehearsal
This is not the project for you if —

Your instinct is always to play more. If your idea of a great drummer is measured purely in speed and volume, or if you've never worked in a studio environment and aren't interested in doing so, this isn't the right fit — and we'd rather say that clearly than waste either of our time.

Age range: 30s–40s preferred. This band holds one non-negotiable: every human being deserves equal dignity regardless of who they are, who they love, or where they come from. If that's a political statement to you, we're genuinely not the right project.

Guitar · Charlotte NC
The signal needs
texture & teeth.
Lead / rhythm guitarist sought — atmosphere, hooks, and the space between

The guitar in this project does several things at once. It carries melodic weight. It builds atmosphere. It locks into the groove. And occasionally — when the song earns it — it opens up completely. What it never does is fill space for its own sake. We want a player who understands the difference between a part that's there because it's right and a part that's there because silence felt uncomfortable.

If you know the guitar work of Thrice, Nothing But Thieves, Minus The Bear, Copeland, Mae, Young the Giant, and Death Cab — and if what you love about those records is the craft, the restraint, and the way a single well-placed phrase can carry an entire song — then you already hear what we're trying to build.

What this requires
  • Fluency across rhythm and lead — this is not a strictly siloed role
  • Strong feel for texture and atmosphere — effects, space, and layering are part of your vocabulary
  • Studio recording experience. You understand tone, microphone placement, and the difference between live and recorded sound
  • Touring experience — you've carried gear, survived a van, and played the same set on adrenaline and not much else
  • Harmony vocals required. You sing. You sing well enough to contribute to stacked arrangements
  • Charlotte, NC or surrounding areas. In-person rehearsals are non-negotiable for this project
This is not the project for you if —

You want a platform to showcase technical ability above all else. This music is collaborative and song-first. If you find restraint frustrating rather than interesting, or if your experience is purely local with no studio work to show, we're probably not a match.

Age range: 30s–40s preferred. This band holds one non-negotiable: every human being deserves equal dignity regardless of who they are, who they love, or where they come from. If that's a political statement to you, we're genuinely not the right project.

Contact: tristanmatthew@gmail.com · Include links to recordings and a brief introduction

Keys / Synth · Charlotte NC
The signal needs
its frequency.
Keys & synthesizer player sought — the electronic foundation of something atmospheric

This role is the connective tissue of the project. The thing that lives in the spaces between guitar, bass, and drums and makes the whole thing feel larger than its parts. We need a player who is equally comfortable with warm analogue textures, precise electronic programming, piano-rooted melodic work, and knowing instinctively which of those the song needs at any given moment.

If your record collection includes MuteMath, Mew, Massive Attack, Portishead, Copeland, and Mae — and if you've spent time understanding how synth and keys operate not just as an instrument but as a production element and a frequency layer — we need to talk.

What this requires
  • Proficiency across both acoustic/electric piano and synthesizer — this is not one or the other
  • Understanding of sound design — you can create and shape a tone, not just play a preset
  • Familiarity with live electronic performance — loops, triggers, layering in a live context
  • Studio recording experience — you've worked in a professional environment and understand the process
  • Touring experience — live performance history beyond local shows
  • Harmony vocals a significant advantage. This band sings. Being able to contribute matters
  • Charlotte, NC or surrounding areas. Regular in-person presence required
This is not the project for you if —

Your keyboard experience is limited to one domain — piano only, or synth-preset only — without the range to move between them. Equally, if you've never performed live in a serious touring context or worked in a recording studio, this is a significant ask and we'd rather be honest about the gap upfront than discover it later.

Age range: 30s–40s preferred. This band holds one non-negotiable: every human being deserves equal dignity regardless of who they are, who they love, or where they come from. If that's a political statement to you, we're genuinely not the right project.

03 — Concept Explainer

In-World Version:
For Those Already

Internal Transmission · For Those Being Considered

What The Order of Light Is —
and What It Asks of You

The Music First

The Order of Light is an original alternative rock band based in Charlotte, NC. The music is melodic, atmospheric, harmony-driven, and emotionally direct. It is rooted in the tradition of bands who understood that a great song is a piece of architecture — every element exists to serve the whole. Hooks matter. Space matters. Vocals matter enormously.

The sonic territory sits somewhere in the lineage of Thrice, Copeland, Nothing But Thieves, Minus The Bear, MuteMath, Death Cab for Cutie, Sunny Day Real Estate, Mew, Young the Giant, and Mae. What those records share — melodic ambition, emotional honesty, a refusal to be either too soft or too heavy — is the centre of gravity we're building around. The music has a dark atmosphere but a warm core. Dreamy and grooving. The kind of thing that sounds effortless and is anything but.

There are approximately 40 songs in various stages of development. Three EPs are planned across the first year of release, structured around a thematic arc that gives the project a shape and momentum most bands never achieve before their first album. The material exists. The vision is clear. What's needed now are the right people to bring it to life.

The Mythology — What It Is and Is Not

The project operates under a deliberate artistic identity: The Order of Light. This is a creative and marketing framework, not a belief system. There is no actual religion here, no ideology, no hierarchy of thought that members are expected to adopt. It is a persona — a carefully constructed world that gives the music a context, a mythology, and a visual identity that is distinctive enough to be remembered and resonant enough to mean something.

The mythology draws on the aesthetics of spiritual and philosophical traditions — Buddhist numerology, runic symbolism, the language of transmission and signal — not to promote any of those things, but because they carry weight, depth, and a sense of something larger than a band's usual self-presentation. The Order of Light is a story we are telling around the music. A frame that makes the picture more interesting.

First you're something. Then you're nothing. Now you're everything. 108 · The Three-EP Arc · The Shape of the First Year

The number 108 structures the release arc. In Buddhist tradition, 108 beads on a mala mark the passage from attachment to release — from the bounded self toward something larger. Three EPs across twelve months enact that journey through music: the first grounded in identity and selfhood, the second in dissolution and transformation, the third in expansion and arrival. This is the framework. The songs don't need to explain it — they simply need to belong to it.

The Marketing & Campaign Strategy

The Order of Light launches with the mythology preceding the music by several months. Social media, a fan club (The Illuminated), a physical ordination kit, a fictional spokesperson figure called The Conduit, and a year of carefully planned content all create the world before the band is revealed within it. This is a deliberate slow-build strategy — the opposite of the launch-and-hope model most independent bands use.

The aesthetic references are DHARMA Initiative (LOST), Massive Attack's visual world, the sincerity of cult documentary filmmaking, and early-internet mystery ARGs. Visually striking, emotionally warm, slightly unsettling in the best possible way. The brand has been developed in full — logo, colour system, typography, campaign calendar, content strategy, physical merchandise. It is a complete package. What it needs is the music, and the musicians, at its centre.

What the Project Asks of You

Being in this band means being genuinely committed to the artistic vision. That means understanding and respecting the aesthetic framework — not performing it, but believing in it as a creative context worth inhabiting. It means being a serious, experienced musician who brings professionalism to recording and touring. It means being a collaborator, not a passenger.

It also means being someone who believes — without caveats — that every human being deserves dignity and respect. This is not a political band. We don't have a party. We do have a baseline: every person in this room is equal, full stop. Anyone who finds that principle controversial will find the rest of this project uncomfortable too, and we'd rather be honest about that now. The music belongs to anyone who can hear it. So does this band.

If you're reading this and something in it resonates — if the music, the vision, the ambition, or the ethics of it feels like something you want to be part of — then the signal may already have found you.

The Practical Reality

  • Location: Charlotte, NC. In-person rehearsals. This is not a remote project
  • Experience required: Previous studio recording credits and genuine touring history. Both. Not one or the other
  • Audition process: Submission of existing recorded work, followed by writing to existing material, followed by in-person sessions with the project founder
  • Harmony vocals: Every member of this band sings. Not necessarily lead, but harmonies are part of the live and recorded sound. This is a requirement, not a preference
  • Timeline: The campaign infrastructure is built. The mythology launches before the music. Position available now — the right person shapes the sound of what gets recorded
  • Compensation: This is an artist project with a serious long-term commercial plan. Revenue sharing will be discussed in detail with serious candidates

Straight-Talking Version:
Just the Facts

Band Information Brief · Charlotte, NC · 2025
The Order of Light —
What This Project Is

The Short Version

The Order of Light is a new original rock band based in Charlotte, NC. We are currently recruiting musicians — vocalist, drummer, guitarist, and keys/synth player — to complete the lineup. We are looking for experienced, serious adults in their 30s–40s who have actually been in recording studios and on tour. If that's not you, this isn't the right project.

The Music

Melodic alternative rock. Atmospheric, harmony-heavy, groove-forward. The kind of music that's sonically interesting but never loses sight of the song. Reference points include Thrice, Nothing But Thieves, Copeland, Death Cab for Cutie, MuteMath, Minus The Bear, Sunny Day Real Estate, Young the Giant, Mew, Mae, and Counting Crows. Not all of those at once — but that's the family of sound. Very melodic, very hooky, strong on vocals and harmonies. Chilled, dreamy, and grooving rather than heavy.

There are around 40 original songs at various stages of completion. The plan is three EPs across the first year, with a full marketing and release strategy already built and ready to execute.

On the name and visual identity: The Order of Light has a specific aesthetic — dark, atmospheric, slightly mysterious. It's an artistic persona, not a belief system. There is no religion, no philosophy members are expected to adopt. It's a creative identity designed to make the music more interesting and give the band a distinctive presence. If you've ever appreciated how a band like Nine Inch Nails or Massive Attack built a world around their music, you'll understand the approach immediately.

What's Already Built

  • Full brand identity — logo, colour system, visual guidelines, all designed and documented
  • 12-month campaign plan — social media strategy, content calendar, release schedule, all mapped out
  • Fan club structure (The Illuminated) — physical merchandise concept, membership tiers, Discord community framework
  • ~40 original songs in development across three EP's worth of material
  • Bass player already in place
  • The infrastructure to launch seriously from day one

What We Need From You

  • Previous studio recording experience — professional sessions, not just home demos
  • Real touring history — more than local shows; you've been on the road
  • The ability to sing harmonies — every member of this band contributes to the vocal arrangements
  • Willingness to audition by submitting recorded material and writing to existing tracks
  • To be based in Charlotte, NC or genuinely able to commit to regular in-person rehearsals
  • To be a collaborative adult who can take direction and give it in equal measure

Who This Band Is For

Anyone who is serious about music, has the experience to back it up, and wants to be part of something with a real vision and a real plan behind it. We are not interested in casual participation or musicians who have been meaning to get back into it seriously. We are interested in people who have never really stopped.

This band is open to every person regardless of background, gender identity, sexual orientation, or ethnicity. What we ask in return is straightforward: be talented, be experienced, be professional, and treat every person in the room with basic human decency. We are not a political band and have no interest in becoming one. We do believe, without negotiation, that every human being deserves equal dignity. That's not a left or right position to us — it's simply the minimum requirement for being in a room together. If that's a sticking point, this isn't the right project.

The Audition Process

  • Step 1: Send an introduction email with links to your best recorded work. If you have nothing recorded, we can't move forward
  • Step 2: If your recordings show the right fit, you'll receive existing tracks to write and record to — a short demo demonstrating your feel for the material
  • Step 3: In-person session in Charlotte, NC. This is where chemistry either exists or it doesn't

Contact

Send an introduction to tristanmatthew@gmail.com — your background, your experience, your instrument, and what draws you to this project.

Every message is read. Response within 48 hours for serious inquiries.
