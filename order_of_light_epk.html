Biography

Two versions — short for listings and social, long for features and editorial.

50 words

The Order of Light are an alternative rock band from Charlotte, NC. Atmospheric and melodic, their music sits in the lineage of Thrice, Copeland, and Nothing But Thieves — dark at the edges, warm at its core. Their debut release arrives via First Broadcast.

200 words

The Order of Light are an alternative rock band from Charlotte, NC, operating under their own label First Broadcast. Their music is melodic, atmospheric, and emotionally direct — built on harmony, groove, and the conviction that a song should move you before it explains itself.

The sonic territory spans the lineage of Thrice, Copeland, Nothing But Thieves, Minus The Bear, MuteMath, and Death Cab for Cutie: melodic ambition, emotional honesty, and a refusal to be either too soft or too heavy. Dark atmospherically, warm at its core. Dreamy, grooving, and deliberately intentional in every detail.

The project operates under a constructed mythology — a visual and narrative world built around the music rather than behind it. The mythology is a frame, not a belief system. It gives the audience something to find and something to return to.

Their debut release is a three-EP arc — three transmissions across twelve months, structured around a thematic journey. The campaign, brand identity, and fan community infrastructure are fully built and ready to execute.

03

The Framework

For journalists and editorial — what makes this project distinctive.

The Order of Light is a fully constructed artistic world — mythology, visual identity, and narrative arc built around the music. The project uses an AI-generated fictional spokesperson (The Conduit) whose video Broadcasts form the mythology's public face. The members remain anonymous. The world speaks for itself.
The Mythology
An AI-generated Conduit delivers the mythology as a series of video Broadcasts — a fictional spokesperson whose identity is never revealed. The world is built before the audience arrives.
The Release Arc
Three EPs across twelve months — titled Something, Nothing, Everything — enacting a journey from identity through dissolution to expansion. The number 108 structures the arc.
The Frequency
An annual collaboration series: one song sent as a bare chart and lyric sheet to three artists — established, mid-level, and emerging. Each records independently. The results release in sequence. No one hears the others' versions until they're published.
The Illuminated
A tiered fan membership community — early access, exclusive content, and a physical ordination kit for founding members. Designed as a community, not a mailing list.
